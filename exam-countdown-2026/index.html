PSLE Science Study Plan 2026
Week-by-week roadmap calibrated to the SEAB timetable · Today is 14 May 2026
🔥
May – June 2026 (NOW — 13 Weeks to Written Papers)
Deep Content Revision
Topic by topic — work through all 15 PSLE topics systematically, 1–2 per week
Food chains & webs — producers, consumers, predator-prey, food web disruption
Life cycles — complete vs incomplete metamorphosis, plant reproduction
Energy forms — conversion, conservation, the updated 2026 syllabus energy topics
Electricity & circuits — series, parallel, current, voltage, open/closed
ScienceStar quizzes — do the topic quiz after every topic. Track your score.
June holidays intensive — 3 full PSLE-style papers during June school break
🗣️
July 2026 (6 Weeks to Oral, 10 Weeks to Science)
Oral Prep + Science Weak Areas
Oral drills daily — read aloud 10 min every morning, practise stimulus responses
Science — Booklet B focus — open-ended questions, fair test, data interpretation
Diagram labelling — respiratory system, digestive system, flower parts, water cycle
Past year questions — 2–3 questions per topic from the PYQ bank
🎧
12–13 Aug: ORAL · Aug–Sep 2026 (5 Weeks to Written)
Post-Oral Science Sprint
After Oral: full Science focus — one full topic revision + practice daily
15 Sep: LC exam — practise listening skills, active note-taking from audio
Timed full papers — 2 complete PSLE Science papers under exam conditions
Mark & correct — every wrong answer: write out the model answer 3 times
OEQ strategy — practise keyword answers: "because", "therefore", "this causes"
🚀
15 Sep → 29 Sep 2026 (Final 2 Weeks)
Final Science Push
Revise all 15 topics in 10 days — 1.5 topics per day using the ScienceStar notes
Flash cards — key definitions and processes for every topic
No new content after 25 Sep — consolidation only
28 Sep eve — light revision, early night. Science paper is 08:15 on 29 Sep

O Level Science Study Plan 2026
Phase-by-phase roadmap from now to 10 November · Today is 14 May 2026
📖
May – Jun 2026 (NOW — 18 Weeks to Practicals)
Full Syllabus Content Completion
Finish all syllabus content — no gaps going into Term 3. Use school notes + ScienceStar.
Physics — Kinematics, Forces, Pressure, Energy, Thermal, Waves, Electricity, Magnetism
Chemistry — Atomic structure, Bonding, Moles, Acids/Bases, Organic, Qualitative Analysis
Biology — Cell biology, Enzymes, Transport, Respiration, Ecology, Reproduction, Genetics
June holidays — 2-week intensive. Complete first round of past papers for each science.
ScienceStar cheat sheets — study the formula and definition sheets for each topic
⚗️
Jul – Sep 2026 (Practical Prep · Oral on 13–14 Jul)
Practical Skills + Deep Revision
13–14 Jul: English Oral — prepare by practising speaking naturally about science topics and everyday issues
Practical lab skills — titration, microscopy, electricity experiments, qualitative analysis tables
Practise drawing labelled diagrams — heart, lungs, kidney, plant cells under exact O Level standards
Structured questions — 6–10 marks answers. Practise clear, concise, point-by-point format
MCQ speed drills — Paper 1 is 30 MCQ in 60 min. Aim for 1.5 min per question
🔬
30 Sep – 13 Oct 2026 · PRACTICAL EXAMS WEEK
Science Practicals — Stay Sharp
30 Sep: Chemistry Practical — qualitative analysis, titration, systematic observations
5 Oct: Physics Practical — measurements, electricity circuits, graph plotting from data
8 Oct: Combined Sci Practical — know which combination you are sitting (Phy/Chem, etc.)
13 Oct: Biology Practical — microscopy, food tests, enzyme experiments, biological drawings
Between practicals — light written paper revision. Don't neglect theory while doing practicals.
🚀
19 Oct – 10 Nov 2026 · WRITTEN EXAM SEASON
Final Written Paper Push
19 Oct: English starts — keep Science revision going alongside English week
27–29 Oct: Science P2 week — Chemistry (27), Biology (28), Physics (29). Highest marks here.
3 Nov: Physics + Chemistry P1 MCQ — last chance to sharpen theory. No new studying, only review.
10 Nov: Biology + Combined Sci P1 — final exam. Revise cell biology, genetics, ecology.
Each paper eve — light revision of key definitions + formulas only. Sleep by 10pm.
